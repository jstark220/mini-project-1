pcaobus.org — Inspection Reports & Findings
Sachiel's Take

FASB January 2025 ~8 min read

ASU 2023-09: Improvements to Income Tax Disclosures — What Changes and Why It Matters

fasb.org — Accounting Standards Update
Sachiel's Take

ASU 2023-09 is one of the more consequential disclosure updates in recent years for public companies. The disaggregated rate reconciliation requirement in particular will demand significantly more work from tax and financial reporting teams — but it gives investors far better tools to understand effective tax rate drivers. This is a good example of FASB listening to investors over preparers, and I think it is the right call. Effective for fiscal years beginning after December 15, 2024 for public companies — meaning audit teams will be dealing with this immediately.

Harvard Business Review December 2024 ~15 min read

The Hidden Costs of Quarterly Earnings Guidance: Evidence from Long-Term Investment Decisions

hbr.org — Harvard Business Review
Sachiel's Take

The short-termism debate is evergreen, but this piece brings solid empirical grounding to what is often a philosophical argument. The evidence that quarterly guidance correlates with underinvestment in R&D and capital expenditures is not surprising — but the magnitude is. From an audit and financial reporting perspective, this also raises questions about how management bias in estimate-setting might interact with earnings guidance targets. Worth reading alongside anything on the SEC's push for more forward-looking MD&A disclosures.

Journal of Accountancy November 2024 ~10 min read

Going Concern in a Post-Pandemic World: Updated Considerations for Auditors Under AU-C Section 570

journalofaccountancy.com — Journal of Accountancy
Sachiel's Take

Going concern has always been one of the more judgment-intensive areas of audit — and the macro environment of the past few years has made it harder, not easier. This piece does a good job laying out how auditors should be thinking about the 12-month look-forward period when clients are operating with elevated debt loads and uncertain rate environments. The discussion of mitigating factors and management's plans is particularly useful for anyone preparing for the CPA exam's AUD section, where going concern comes up frequently.

SEC.gov October 2024 ~20 min read

SEC Final Rule: The Enhancement and Standardization of Climate-Related Disclosures for Investors

sec.gov — Final Rules & Regulations
Sachiel's Take

The SEC's climate disclosure rule — even in its scaled-back form following court challenges — is probably the most consequential change to public company reporting in a generation. The Scope 1 and 2 emissions disclosures for large accelerated filers, combined with attestation requirements, will pull auditors and accountants into sustainability reporting in a serious way. I am watching this closely both as someone entering audit and as someone interested in how capital markets price ESG risk. The rule's ultimate fate is still uncertain, but the direction of travel is clear.
